The Problem

Most sprinkler systems still water by the clock, not environmental reality

Traditional sprinklers are usually controlled by fixed timers. They turn on at the same time each day, regardless of whether the soil is already moist or whether significant rain is expected tomorrow.

Why this matters
The Solution

An intelligent irrigation system that responds to the environment

Instead of relying on a simple timer, the system runs on weather forecasts, data from moisture sensors and AI forecasts. Based on this, it computes a dynamic sprinkling schedule, customized for each watering zone, and operating in the best time of the day given your geographical region.

Key Insight

Different places need different watering logic

In Southern California, for example, the best time to water is often late at night or very early in the morning to reduce evaporation. In upstate New York, on the other hand, watering right after sunrise may be more effective. This project combines environmental awareness with local timing rules, making the system flexible across different climates.

SoCal

Drought-first logic

Prioritize low-evaporation watering windows and careful water budgeting.

Upstate New York

Rain-aware logic

Adapt to rapidly changing weather and avoid watering when rain is near.

About Me

I am a 9th grade student at Ithaca High School, and I recently moved to upstate New York from Southern California. In California, water conservation and irrigation are major concerns, since people want to maintain green landscapes in what is basically a desert climate.

Here in upstate New York, I have experienced a completely different environment, with frequent rain and rapidly changing weather. This contrast has shown me how wasteful the systems we use in SoCal would be in this climate. While drought is not a major concern in Ithaca and watering consumes relatively little energy, I believe the lessons from this project can have far greater impact elsewhere.
